Procrastination. The bane of your academic pursuance. You have yet again come across a trial of desperate time management, in which you must complete your tasks properly before dawn - with a suitable time left to rest for the morning.

On this 9PM evening, what lays before you is a 10 page essay you haven't started nor planned for, with a morning deadline with no exception for lateness, and due to your tardiness in the class, you need a "B" on this paper if you want to pass the class.

Other assignments collectively require your hand in finalizing - which luckily you only need probably an hour at most to do so.

The devil is in the details. All this time, for all your work, and enough for your needed respite.

What should you start first?

After 30 minutes of research and 2 hours of writing, you've orchestrated a paper of absolute drivel...or bullcrap, as your teacher would describe after a sarcastic sniffing gesture, followed with the phrase,"Why is this the first thing I smell in the morning?" With the quick references you managed to scrounge up, you've basically written the same arguments with only some expanded ideas differentiating them...for ten pages straight.

It is now 11:30PM.

You know this isn't up to snuff - not even to pass as good bullcrap covered in the scent of roses.

You still have an all-nighter to pull for the assignments, but if you want a good mark on this paper you'll need to...


Salvage Essay or Revise Essay

During your hour of research, you managed to make a nice outline of correlating ideas, relevant research references, and a strong thesis to help make the 2 hour long writing a little easier. With a clear outline, you had enough brevity to consistently write thoughtout arguments with research to back it up, and sum it up nicely with the main ideas intact.

It is now 12AM. You could go with your gut on a pretty okay essay, but it would be even more satisfying had you just another 30 minutes to properly edit; besides, the assignments only need an hour to finish up anyway, and getting to sleep at 2AM would be enough.

So...what'll it be?
